About Tutor Log

Built by tutors who needed a better way to remember what was going on with every student.

Why we made it

Tutor Log was built by a family of tutors.

We all had our own way of keeping track of students. One of us used Word documents. One relied on WhatsApp messages and had to scroll back every week. One used scribbles in a notebook.

It worked, but only just.

The problem was not the teaching. The problem was remembering all the little things around the teaching: what happened last lesson, what homework was set, what the student struggled with, what needed to be sent later, and what to pick up next time.

What Tutor Log does

Tutor Log turns quick lesson notes into a tidy student history.

You type what happened in the lesson, even if it is messy. Tutor Log helps organise it into a student timeline, memory snapshot, to-do list and parent update.

So when the next lesson comes around, you are not relying on memory, old messages, or random notes. You can quickly see what happened last time and what needs to happen next.

Not a worksheet machine

We included a resource library for useful websites, books and tools.

But Tutor Log is not trying to generate endless worksheets. There are already good resources out there. The harder bit is remembering what you used, what worked, and what each student needs next.

Less unpaid admin

Tutor Log can also turn your lesson note into a short parent update, ready to copy and send.

When tutoring is charged by the hour, the admin around it should not take over your evening. A quick summary keeps parents informed without making every lesson spill into unpaid time.

Small things, kept in one place

Each student also has a to-do list, so you can quickly add things like sending homework, marking a paper, printing a worksheet, chasing a parent, or following up next lesson.

Nothing too complicated. Just a simple way to stop important little things from disappearing.

The point

Tutor Log is not here to replace tutors.

It is here so tutors do not have to keep every student in their head.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Tutor Log?

Tutor Log is a simple student memory system for tutors. You type a quick lesson note, and Tutor Log helps organise it into a clear student timeline, memory snapshot, to-do list and parent update.

Who is Tutor Log for?

Tutor Log was built mainly for private tutors, but it can also be useful for online tutors, student tutors, 11+ tutors, GCSE tutors, A-level tutors, language tutors, music tutors and classroom teachers who want a simple way to keep track of students over time.

How do I use it after a lesson?

Open the student’s profile, type a quick note into “What happened today?”, then click “Save & create update.” Tutor Log organises your note into the student’s timeline, updates the memory snapshot, creates any useful to-dos and drafts a parent update.

Do my notes need to be tidy?

No. Tutor Log is designed for quick notes, scribbles and brain dumps. You can write in natural language, bullet points or short phrases. Add the topic, score, homework, parent reminders, cancellations or anything else you want Tutor Log to remember.

What does Tutor Log do with my lesson note?

Tutor Log reads your note and pulls out useful details, such as topics covered, scores, homework, next steps, parent updates and student-specific tasks. If something appears repeatedly across recent lessons, Tutor Log can also help surface it as a pattern. Your original note is still saved, so you can always go back to exactly what you wrote.

What is the Memory Snapshot?

The Memory Snapshot gives you a quick view of the important things Tutor Log has noticed or remembered about a student. This can include recurring weak areas, recent scores, current resources, parent contact details, custom tracking notes and anything else that helps you pick up smoothly next lesson.

What should I put in “What should Tutor Log remember?”

Use this section to tell Tutor Log what matters for that particular student. For example: “Track 10-minute test scores,” “remember textbook page progress,” “watch for careless mistakes,” or “track confidence with algebra.”

Can I add and edit students?

Yes. You can add a student with their name, year group, subject, goal or exam, parent contact details and any custom things you want Tutor Log to remember. You can also edit or delete a student later.

What is the Student timeline?

The Student timeline is a tidy history of that student’s lessons. It shows what happened, what was covered, what homework was set, what needs to happen next and any parent update created from the lesson note.

What is the To-do list for?

The To-do list is for small student-related tasks you do not want to forget. For example: send homework, mark a paper, chase a parent, print a worksheet, check an exam date or follow something up before the next lesson.

Can Tutor Log create parent updates?

Yes. Tutor Log can turn your lesson note into a short parent-friendly update, so you can communicate clearly without writing everything from scratch after every lesson.

Does Tutor Log send messages to parents automatically?

No. Tutor Log drafts the parent update for you, but you choose whether to copy, edit and send it yourself. That keeps you in control of what parents receive.

Is Tutor Log an AI tutor?

No. Tutor Log is not here to replace tutors, teach students or make professional decisions for you. It is a memory and admin assistant that helps organise what you already know about your students.

Is Tutor Log a full CRM or invoicing system?

No. Tutor Log is intentionally lightweight. It includes simple tools for student notes, to-dos, parent updates and basic payment tracking, but it is not a full agency management system, calendar platform, invoicing platform or accounting tool.

Can I track payments or cancellations?

Yes. Tutor Log includes a simple payments ledger and balance tracker, so you can record payments, lesson deductions and basic payment notes. It is useful for tutor admin, but it is not a replacement for proper accounting records.

What is the Resource library?

The Resource library is a simple list of useful websites, books and tools for tutors. It is there to help you quickly access good resources, not to replace your own judgement or generate endless worksheets.

Does Tutor Log create worksheets?

No. There are already lots of good resources out there. Tutor Log is more focused on helping you remember what happened, what worked, what each student needs next and what you need to follow up.

Do I need to download an app?

No. Tutor Log is a web app, so you can use it in your browser on a laptop, tablet or phone.

Is Tutor Log free?

Tutor Log is currently in beta and using a free tier. Pricing may change later as the product develops, but the aim is to keep it affordable for individual tutors.

Is student data private?

Tutor Log is designed for tutor notes and student records, so you should only add information that is useful and appropriate for tutoring. Avoid adding unnecessary sensitive details. Your notes are used to provide your own lesson summaries, student memory, to-dos and parent updates.

Can I use Tutor Log for classroom teaching?

Yes, although it was originally designed for private tutors. Teachers may also find it useful for keeping quick notes on students, preparing for parents’ evening, tracking patterns, or remembering small follow-ups across a class.
